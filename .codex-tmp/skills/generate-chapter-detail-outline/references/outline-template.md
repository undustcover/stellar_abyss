## Scene Count Rule

Use scene count as a pacing decision, not a completion quota. For a typical 3500-character web-novel chapter, default to 3-5 effective scenes. Use 1-2 core scenes when the chapter is deliberately concentrated around one emotional pressure point, dialogue confrontation, reveal, or location. Expand to 5-7 light scenes only when the chapter outline clearly requires a longer chapter, fast cuts, multiple threads, action escalation, or several necessary turns. Keep each scene lightweight enough that a later chapter execution pack can preserve whole-chapter flow.

## Prose Boundary

For `对白重点`, write intent and topic, not full lines. For `主要行动`, write action beats, not descriptive paragraphs.

## New Prose-Generation Bridge Rule

The outline must not write final prose, but it must prepare the later execution pack and prose draft for human-like narration. Always include:

- `章节文风模式`: inherit the chapter-outline style mode; if absent, mark a gap instead of inventing certainty.
- `人物语言调用`: list the relevant character-card language fields and what the chapter must not let each character say.
- `场景文风分配`: split style and emotional weight by scene.
- `本章认知差控制`: separate viewpoint knowledge, reader knowledge, support-character knowledge, and hidden information.
- `场景轻重与承压点`: identify core pressure scenes and what cannot be removed during later compression.
- `生活压力链`: concrete life pressure, objects, procedures, relationships, or money/process details that make the chapter feel lived-in.
- `误会语义链`: surface meaning, true meaning, and how prose should preserve the misread.
- `人物情绪链`: emotion, purpose, and speech shape for key characters.
- `反杀材料链`: light surface action or object that can later become evidence, consequence, or emotional reversal.
- `读者体验曲线`: expectation, misread, pressure, laugh, reveal, and hook as the chapter is read.
- `场景导演目标`: scene-level handling of style, humor, anti-AI feel, pressure protection, and word-budget role.

## System-Source Boundary

The outline must auto-generate structure and opportunities from allowed system inputs. It may write that a character's wrong social experience should slide a family/workplace conversation toward a misleading service or reception context, but it must not write finished punchlines, finished dialogue, exact scene order copied from external prose, or recognizable expressions from non-system writing material.
