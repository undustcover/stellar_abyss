# Draft To Public Quality Gates

All three gates must pass.

## 1. Fact And Boundary Gate

- Core events unchanged.
- Scene functions and results unchanged.
- Character decisions, abilities, and relationship states unchanged.
- POV and knowledge boundaries unchanged.
- No unauthorized facts.
- Required tension, uncertainty, ending function, and continuation interface preserved.

Any failure stops prose generation. Do not patch it with invention.

## 2. Author Aesthetic Gate

- Information enters through characters, actions, objects, relations, or purposeful narration.
- Dialogue is not a cooperative information checklist.
- Narration adds value instead of repeating visible conclusions.
- Character qualities are supported by choices and consequences.
- Formulaic metaphors have a real experiential anchor or are removed.
- The primary tone is explicit; secondary tones do not receive equal mechanical weight.
- The text stops after sufficient material and preserves reader participation.
- Supporting jokes or characters do not steal the main scene function.

## 3. Modification Depth Gate

### L2

- Sentence structure is genuinely rewritten.
- The change is not only synonym replacement or shorter line breaks.

### L3

- At least two relationships among action, dialogue, narration, psychology, order, and information entry change.
- The new paragraph does not map sentence-by-sentence to the Draft.

### L4

- The fragment is written from a constraint card, not the original paragraph.
- At least two information-entry types are used.
- Character purposes change dialogue behavior.
- At least one original explanatory conclusion is carried by the scene.
- Every frozen item passes.

## Failure Rules

- Redo only the failed fragment.
- Keep the same facts and gates during retest.
- Attribute the failure to one primary module.
- Update knowledge when the method is missing.
- Update the Skill when a confirmed method was not executed.
- Update the template when the failure could not be recorded or gated.
- Return upstream when the failure belongs to plot, intent, or scene design.
