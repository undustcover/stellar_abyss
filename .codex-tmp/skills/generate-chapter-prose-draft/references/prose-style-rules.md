# Prose Style Rules

Use these rules while drafting chapter prose.

## Write the Scene, Not the Outline

- Start from a concrete present-moment action, object, voice, pressure, or consequence.
- Do not summarize what the scene is for.
- Do not explain the chapter plan to the reader.
- Do not simply expand the outline scene by scene. First make the scene move through concrete life pressure, character reaction, misread, action consequence, and the next pressure.

## New Author-Style Controls

- Build a `生活压力链`: work, family, money, procedure, objects, messages, meals, doors, forms, calls, or relationship pressure should make the chapter feel lived-in.
- Build a `误会语义链`: important clues should have a surface meaning the viewpoint can misread and a true meaning that remains hidden.
- Build a `人物情绪链`: dialogue is driven first by emotion, then purpose, then logic.
- Build a `反杀材料链`: light early actions or objects can later become evidence, consequence, or emotional reversal, but do not label them as such in prose.
- Follow the execution pack's `分场景导演表`: each scene should know what reader expectation, misread, pressure, laugh, reveal, or hook it is carrying before prose is written.
- Follow scene grades: A scenes get full visible pressure; B scenes bridge state; C scenes compress information through conflict or procedure.
- Generate concrete joke landings, dialogue, objects, and ending lines during drafting. Do not import them from external prose drafts, comparison reports, finished sample text, or other non-system writing material.

## Integrated Scene Director

- De-AI feel, style consistency, and dialogue humor are not separate later filters. They are scene-level drafting responsibilities.
- Before writing a scene, identify the one primary reader effect it must create. Cut or compress material that does not serve that effect.
- Keep the prose from becoming a rephrased outline by making each scene turn on action, pressure, object, money/procedure, relationship, or information mismatch.
- If a scene fails, mark it for local replacement. Do not rewrite the whole chapter unless several core scenes fail for the same structural reason.
- Control token and word drift by spending drafting density only where the execution pack marks an A scene.

## Revision Triage

For a revision or second-pass draft, internally sort material before rewriting:

- `保留`: anything carrying chapter function, character turn, misread semantics, scene pressure, reversal material, or the main hook.
- `压缩`: useful material with too much line density or polish. Reduce expression, not the pressure point.
- `删除`: lines that are only clever, pretty, or funny but do not change the scene.
- `延后`: truth, mechanism, long-term conflict, or full backstory that the chapter does not need to settle.

Revision is not just adding or cutting. It is choosing the few pressures that should remain visible.

## Preserve Cognition

- Keep viewpoint perception and inference inside the character's current knowledge.
- If the execution pack allows reader tension beyond the character's knowledge, show it through surface clues, pressure, absence, or dramatic irony.
- Do not let hidden mechanics become narrator explanation.
- Keep important support-character knowledge separate from viewpoint knowledge. A support character may act from fear, shame, anger, or control before the viewpoint understands why.

## Show Character Through Behavior

- Show character through action, delay, refusal, compromise, silence, habitual response, and dialogue pressure.
- Avoid authorial diagnosis of personality or motivation when behavior can carry it.
- Do not convert upstream card constraints into visible process wording like "写作禁忌" or "设定约束" inside prose.

## Setting and Mechanism Exposure

- Do not lecture setting rules.
- Present mechanisms through obstruction, cost, procedure, trace, consequence, misread signal, or contradiction.
- If a character does not understand a mechanism, the prose may show only what they can notice, suffer, misread, or infer.

## Dialogue

- Use dialogue to pressure, evade, test, expose contradiction, or reveal relationship.
- Do not make dialogue explain all background.
- Keep speakers inside their knowledge, motive, status, and fear.
- Key conversations should contain natural breaks: interruption, repair after saying too much, silence, answer-not-matching-question, topic dodge, or both people pursuing different urgent purposes.
- Characters should not cooperate to deliver clean information. Each person protects face, avoids pressure, seeks help, pushes blame, or tries to control the relationship.
- Control line win-rate. A strong character may be sharp, but should not win every exchange. After one or two strong lines, shift to action, silence, being interrupted, being wrong, having to repair, or letting another character's pressure take over.
- A sharp line must push action, expose relationship pressure, deepen a misread, or guard information. If it is only clever, cut or roughen it.
- Important support characters need an emotional turn. They should not remain only helpers, explainers, or joke receivers.

## Anti-AI Prose

- Keep logic underneath the scene; do not float it to the surface as explanation.
- Avoid authorial diagnosis of motives immediately after actions.
- Avoid polished aphorisms, neat metaphors, and every paragraph landing like a slogan.
- Prefer rough judgment, concrete object, awkward pause, practical action, or relationship pressure.
- Information must be forced out by pursuit, misunderstanding, conflict, procedure, consequence, or necessity.
- Environment description must do work: misread object, action prop, joke participant, pressure signal, or consequence trace.
- When removing summary humor, do not replace it with a prettier summary. Convert it into a concrete action, object, procedural mismatch, relationship reaction, or broken line of dialogue.
- Keep misreads tactful and double-coded. Do not repeatedly spell out vulgar or blunt interpretations when hesitation, official wording, formal procedure, or an avoided question can carry the joke.

## System-Source Boundary

- The draft is generated from the execution pack, narrative system, setting system, event database, and approved common rule files.
- Do not reuse finished jokes, finished dialogue, punchlines, paragraph order, ending lines, or signature expressions from non-system writing material.
- If the execution pack contains a structural phrase, transform it into new in-scene action or dialogue instead of copying it as prose.
- Approved common rule files may guide rhythm, pressure, misread, and anti-AI control, but non-system finished material must stay out of the draft.

## Web-Novel Chapter Flow

- Preserve momentum.
- Compress bridge actions.
- Spend words on conflict, turn, reveal, emotional pressure, and ending hook.
- Let transitions carry state from the previous scene rather than restarting the chapter.
- Compress jokes before compressing pressure. Do not skip a high-pressure confrontation if it is where a character's face, money, object, procedure, or relationship changes.
- Use the execution pack's protected pressure points as minimum visible beats. If a scene must be short, keep the pressure point and cut the decorative cleverness.

## Ending

- Land the execution pack's chapter ending state.
- Preserve the next-chapter hook without explaining future plot.
- Identify one main ending hook. Comic afterbeats, emotional reflections, and character jokes may remain only if they do not steal attention from that hook.
- The final reader impression should point to the main hook first. If a lighter afterbeat is funnier than the hook, shorten it or move it before the hook.
