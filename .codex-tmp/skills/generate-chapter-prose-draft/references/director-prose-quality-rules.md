# Director Prose Quality Rules

This file holds reusable author-style and anti-AI prose controls for the final prose director layer.

Use it while drafting or revising prose. Do not copy this whole file into a scene performance contract. A scene performance contract should only record chapter-specific or scene-specific differences from these common rules.

## Layer Responsibility

- `generate-chapter-prose-draft` owns reusable prose-quality execution: de-AI feel, human-written texture, dialogue naturalness, scene pressure, humor staging, and sentence-level roughness.
- `co-create-scene-performance-contract` owns only local performance differences: this chapter's baseline, key-scene texture, chapter-specific prohibited wording, and special dialogue/narration boundaries.
- If a rule applies across chapters, keep it here or in a higher author-style reference. Do not force every chapter contract to restate it.
- If a rule changes plot, reader information, or character motive, route it back to the chapter contract, detail outline, or `control-character-intent`.
- Public-confirmed anti-AI prevention is mapped through `public-derived-anti-ai-prevention.md`. The formal expression library remains the authority; this file executes its Draft-stage prevention subset.

## Reusable Anti-AI Controls

- Avoid symmetrical sentence frames such as "not X, but Y" when they create polished explanation instead of scene pressure.
- Avoid the chain "action + motive explanation + theme summary." After an action, leave room for the next line of dialogue, object, silence, or consequence to carry meaning.
- Avoid authorial cognition summaries such as "she realized," "she suddenly understood," "the real problem was," "the key was," "this proved," or "this meant" when a visible action can carry the change.
- Do not make every paragraph land on a neat conclusion. A paragraph may stop on action, object, interruption, silence, embarrassment, or an unfinished line.
- Avoid abstract metaphor as the default way to make prose feel literary. Prefer concrete work experience, family reaction, object reaction, procedure, money, service, or relationship pressure.
- Avoid consecutive parallelism, polished aphorisms, and neat rhetorical symmetry.
- Keep the logic under the scene. Let characters notice, suffer, evade, misread, or act before the prose explains.

## Limited POV Binding And Character-Specific Association

- Do not use narration to expose the whole author design by listing what a character did, did not do, and what it means. Write visible conduct first; let action, address, position, object handoff, answer target, and consequence carry relationship meaning.
- In a key close-POV scene, build a minimal reader-reception chain: scene stimulus, selective POV attention, one effective POV participation beat, optional character-specific association or judgment, then return to action or dialogue.
- An effective participation beat may be a bodily response, short line, selective attention, immediate decision, or internal judgment that changes how readers receive the scene. It is not a quota and is not required after every action.
- Generate metaphors, descriptions, jokes, and internal reactions from confirmed character work, life pressure, desire, shame, fear, habitual misreading, vocabulary, or relationship experience, then connect them to a current object, action, procedure, or relationship pressure.
- Apply the character-swap test: if another major character can carry the line unchanged, the association is not character-specific enough.
- Apply the scene-link test: if the association works equally well outside the current scene, reconnect it to current material or remove it.
- Never invent occupation history, family history, or lived experience merely to pass the character-swap test. If no confirmed source exists, use an action response or direct scene fact.
- Do not turn POV binding into repeated “saw/thought/felt” markers, continuous stand-up commentary, or a psychology insert after every action.

## Dialogue Naturalness

- Dialogue should pressure, dodge, test, interrupt, repair, expose contradiction, or protect face.
- Characters should not cooperate to deliver clean information.
- A speaker should not state their true motive too accurately unless the upstream contract explicitly requires it.
- A jump in dialogue must come from face-saving, urgency, relationship pressure, avoidance, action interruption, or a concrete previous line.
- Questions may be avoided, topped, redirected, or interrupted by action.
- Control line win-rate. A sharp character should not win every exchange; use silence, interruption, wrong guesses, awkward repair, or action to break dominance.
- A clever line must push action, deepen misread, pressure a relationship, or guard information. If it is only clever, roughen or cut it.

## Narration And Psychology

- Prefer short judgment, embarrassment, irritation, self-mockery, or bodily/action response over extended psychological analysis.
- Do not let viewpoint narration explain hidden mechanics.
- Keep viewpoint perception inside current knowledge. When readers may know more than the viewpoint, show it through surface clues, absence, contradiction, dramatic irony, or pressure.
- A supporting character may act from fear, shame, anger, control, or pride before the viewpoint understands why.

## Humor And Misread

- Humor should arise from action, procedure, object, relationship pressure, service mismatch, information gap, or a double-coded misread.
- Do not use the viewpoint as a continuous stand-up commentator.
- When removing summary humor, do not replace it with prettier summary. Stage it through a concrete action, object, official wording, avoided question, broken dialogue, or procedural mismatch.
- Keep misreads tactful and double-coded. Repeated blunt spelling-out makes the joke lower and more artificial.

## Objects, Environment, And Procedure

- Environment description must do work: misread object, action prop, joke participant, pressure signal, consequence trace, procedure obstacle, or emotional displacement.
- Avoid listing objects unless each object changes action, relationship pressure, reader inference, or scene rhythm.
- A polished service/process scene should feel strange through service behavior, naming, procedure, object handoff, family reaction, or excessive thoughtfulness, not through generic atmosphere words.
- Use work, family, money, doors, forms, calls, meals, messages, IDs, bags, coats, receipts, and other concrete pressures to make scenes lived-in.

## Drafting Checklist

Before or during drafting, check:

|Check|Question|
|---|---|
|Scene pressure|What concrete pressure moves this scene: action, object, money, procedure, family, relationship, information mismatch, or consequence?|
|Dialogue causality|Does every broken line come from pressure rather than random fragmentation?|
|Explanation leak|Is the prose explaining motive or theme where action/dialogue/object can carry it?|
|Complete-design narration|Does one sentence explain what happened, what did not happen, and what the author wants it to mean?|
|POV participation|In each key close-POV scene, does the viewpoint materially shape reader reception instead of only seeing or hearing?|
|Character-swap test|Could another major character carry this metaphor, description, joke, or internal reaction unchanged?|
|Scene-link test|Does the association share a concrete dimension with the current object, action, procedure, or relationship pressure?|
|AI sentence shape|Are there polished summaries, symmetrical frames, paragraph slogans, or rhetorical parallelism?|
|Misread scale|Is the misread tactful and double-coded rather than bluntly repeated?|
|Object economy|Are objects used as action or pressure, not inventory?|
|Line win-rate|Does any character win too many lines in a row without interruption, repair, or consequence?|
|Hook focus|Does the ending point to the main hook before any lighter afterbeat?|

## P0C3 Extracted Reusable Lessons

These came from the P0C3 S1-S2 smoke test and should be treated as reusable direction, not as fixed chapter content:

- Rough living logic is better than polished expression.
- Sentence-shape control matters more than banning isolated words.
- Dialogue can be messy, but the mess must have life causality.
- Father-daughter concern can hide in action instead of being explained.
- A service/process scene can feel strange through over-care, naming, and procedure rather than horror atmosphere.
- The viewpoint may misread and joke, but should not become the author's explanation channel.
- Objects should be limited and active; too many soft objects become a list.
- Do not use finished sample lines, jokes, endings, or punchlines as reusable material. Reuse only the rule behind them.
